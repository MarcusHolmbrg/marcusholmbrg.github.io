About the game

The game is a fast paced top down 2D shooter game featuring spells, intense fast paced gameplay and able difficulty to challange the player. The game takes heavy inspiration from the game Hotline Miami but adds its own spin by introducing elements from roguelike games alongside a vastly different setting. Nevertheless, throughout development, we strived to recreate the same addicting gameplay while adding even more intensity and magical chaos.


The gameplay loop consists of the player entering the hub floor on a certain level, after which they are required to aquire a set of crystals to unlock a door that leads to the next hub area. Each of these crystals can be found in various levels accessible through the hub, and requires the player to battle through hordes of enemies trying to stop the player. On their side, the player has a vast array of different spells that can be used to eliminate anyone standing in their way but beware, the enemies use the same spells you do.

Summary of my total contributions for the game demo throughout the project


- Setting up the Unity project/enabling Unity2D in the Unity universal render pipeline.
- Player character movement, controls and animation
- Player camera tracking/panning
- Logic behind all spells used in the game (projectile, AoE, Spells, Utility and blink).
- Modular spell framework containing all spells for the game and allowing for easily adding new spells and spell types.
- Objects used for the spells; projectiles, AoE explosions and their animations, the beam spell visuals and all lightning associated with the spells (Everything except for the base artwork)
- All player UI elements; health/mana bars, spell equip slots and icons, score and timer.
- Main menu; graphics and interaction
- Overarching game logic (level loading and Unity scene management).
- The warden character (Artwork, AI, gameplay logic and conditions for spawning)

I also helped with enemy Ai where I for example made some edits to hook the enemies into the spell framework, making it so that enemies would agro on the player if the player fired a spell nearby as well as adding the option for stationary enemies. (The basis for our enemy Ai script came from a modified version of my previous top down game project) Finally, I also made it so that the enemies would change the color of their robes based on their equipped spell.

Outside of the demo itself, I also participated actively in group meetings and actively partook in discussions around the game demo as well as the business side of things and project planning.


Final Blog Entry

After the final presentation on the 24th I changed my focus to another course were I had a pressing assignment. This was quite problematic, considering that the GDD needed to be ready a week later, but owing to my disproportionatly large contribution to the project up to that point, the other members of the group thought it was only fair they would do a bit more work on the GDD. As such, I took a bit more of a backseat when it came to writing the GDD itself, but I was there for the meetings and actively discussed/provided feedback when requested.

Looking back to the demo, there was a few things that would have been great to improve. One of the things we wanted to add but never had the time was a tutorial section where the player could learn the basics when playing the game. This was especially important when it came to the keybinds but, as we didnt implementing it, we ended up putting the keybinds in an exterior text file.
